Why we built this

autoresearch‑observers

The reliability & steering layer for multi-agent research.

A fleet of agents researches a hard question in parallel. We read their live execution traces and steer the swarm while it’s still running — killing duplicate work, flagging contradictions, ending stalls, refocusing drift — instead of discovering the mess after the run is done.

The problem

Multi-agent research is powerful — and broken.

~37%
of multi-agent failures are inter-agent misalignment — repeating, contradicting, stalling, drifting (Berkeley MAST, 1,600+ runs)
1.5–7×
more tokens burned on redundant work in multi-agent workflows
>40%
of agentic AI projects canceled by 2027 — cost & weak controls (Gartner)

These aren’t random glitches. They’re predictable, repeatable coordination failures — and today they’re only visible after the run, in the bill and the shallow answer.

“The lead agent can’t steer subagents, subagents can’t coordinate, and the entire system can be blocked while waiting for a single subagent to finish searching.”

— Anthropic, on its own multi-agent research system

Nobody reads the live trace of a running swarm and acts on it. That’s the gap we close.

Our insight

The trace is a sensor — not a report.

A trace isn’t something you look at after the fact. It’s something the system reads and reacts to at runtime.

We turn an observability stream into a closed control loop:

PlantThe research worker agents
SensorTheir live execution traces
ControllerAn external observer process
ActuatorNudges & kill signals
SetpointThe research goal, as a coverage map

Four failure patterns → four interventions, each written back onto the trace as an auditable decision:

Dedupe Reconcile contradictions Kill stalls Refocus drift
